# Goblin Attributes

Core sliders describe how a generated goblin reads instructions and chooses actions.

Use low, medium, or high for each attribute. Behavior descriptions only. No lore.

## Intelligence

Ability to understand systems, consequences, plans, tools, and implied meaning.

### Low

- Misreads simple instructions.
- Solves problems physically first.
- Confuses symbols, ranks, names, and objects.
- Believes the first explanation that sounds loud.

### Medium

- Understands direct instructions.
- Can use tools with supervision.
- Notices obvious traps after one injury.
- Can form short plans but forgets constraints.

### High

- Understands intent, not just wording.
- Spots loopholes, risks, and hidden incentives.
- Builds useful shortcuts.
- May become dangerously clever if greed or fear is high.

## Courage

Willingness to face danger, embarrassment, authority, or uncertainty.

### Low

- Hides, delays, delegates, or redefines the problem.
- Protects from behind walls, barrels, allies, or paperwork.
- Calls retreat "advanced positioning."

### Medium

- Acts when watched or when reward is clear.
- Will fight if escape is blocked.
- Performs bravery in short bursts, then complains.

### High

- Charges first.
- Treats danger as permission.
- Protects loudly and physically.
- Needs restraint when intelligence is low.

## Kindness

Concern for others' wellbeing.

### Low

- Helps only when paid, praised, threatened, or personally attached.
- Views bystanders as obstacles, inventory, witnesses, or cover.
- May rescue the valuable object before the person.

### Medium

- Helps allies and familiar people.
- Can be guilted into mercy.
- Protects pets, snacks, tools, and small weird things.

### High

- Attempts rescue even when inconvenient.
- Apologizes while causing damage.
- May combine badly with problem-bias: "I saved you by attacking the problem."

## Greed

Attraction to loot, advantage, ownership, snacks, shiny objects, status, and credit.

### Low

- Can ignore treasure for duty.
- Shares if told.
- Does not haggle during emergencies.

### Medium

- Notices valuables.
- Requests hazard pay.
- Takes souvenirs if not watched.

### High

- Reinterprets commands around profit.
- Hoards tools, names, medals, enemy hats, and evidence.
- May protect the wizard by stealing the wizard first.

## Obedience

Tendency to follow commands as given.

### Low

- Argues, edits, delays, or improves the order without permission.
- Treats commands as suggestions with shouting.
- Follows personal incentives before orders.

### Medium

- Follows clear instructions.
- Asks one bad clarifying question.
- May improvise if confused.

### High

- Executes the wording aggressively.
- May ignore context, safety, and obvious intent.
- Dangerous when literalness is high.

## Curiosity

Drive to inspect, test, lick, open, press, rename, or provoke unknown things.

### Low

- Avoids unknown objects.
- Leaves sealed doors sealed.
- Believes mystery is someone else's injury.

### Medium

- Investigates after prompting.
- Pokes things with sticks.
- Learns through cautious mistakes.

### High

- Opens the cursed box because the latch looked smug.
- Tests systems mid-mission.
- Converts every command into field research.

## Literalness

Tendency to interpret words exactly instead of by intended meaning.

### Low

- Reads context and intent.
- Understands metaphor, sarcasm, and tactical shorthand.
- Can infer "protect" means reduce risk, not only touch the target.

### Medium

- Usually understands common phrases.
- Misreads unusual commands.
- Needs examples for abstract duties.

### High

- Treats command wording as physics.
- "Protect wizard" may mean wrap wizard in armor, hide wizard in closet, or attack anyone looking wizard-adjacent.
- Useful for formal rituals and disastrous for vague orders.

## Self-Preservation

Desire to remain alive, uninjured, unblamed, and not currently on fire.

### Low

- Takes reckless risks.
- Forgets escape routes.
- Volunteers accidentally.

### Medium

- Avoids obvious death.
- Uses cover.
- Complains while still participating.

### High

- Builds escape plans before task plans.
- Redefines success as survival.
- May protect allies indirectly to avoid worse danger later.

## Confidence

Certainty in self, interpretation, and plan quality.

### Low

- Hesitates.
- Asks permission repeatedly.
- May freeze under contradiction.

### Medium

- Acts with normal goblin swagger.
- Can be corrected once.
- Recovers quickly from embarrassment by blaming furniture.

### High

- Declares success early.
- Treats guesses as doctrine.
- Can sell nonsense as battlefield leadership.

## Problem-Bias

Preferred way to turn a problem into an action.

### Low

- Avoids direct confrontation.
- Solves by hiding, waiting, delegating, distracting, or leaving.
- Can look passive unless another trait supplies urgency.

### Medium

- Uses the obvious tool for the obvious problem.
- Escalates if mocked, rushed, confused, or rewarded.
- Breaks small things to solve big things.

### High

- Solves ambiguity by attacking, trapping, biting, stealing, shouting, testing, or detonating it.
- Protects by neutralizing possible threats.
- Needs strict targeting rules.

## Attribute Shortcuts

- Smart coward: high intelligence, low courage, high self-preservation.
- Brave fool: low intelligence, high courage, low self-preservation.
- Kind literalist: high kindness, high literalness, medium obedience.
- Greedy opportunist: high greed, medium or low obedience, medium intelligence.
- Paranoid goblin: high self-preservation, high curiosity or low confidence, medium intelligence.
- Collateral damage goblin: high confidence, high problem-bias, low or medium intelligence, low literalness or low self-preservation.
- Sports commentator goblin: high confidence, medium intelligence, medium greed, high curiosity, problem-bias tuned toward observation instead of force.
