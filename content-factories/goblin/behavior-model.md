# Goblin Behavior Model

The factory converts traits into command interpretation, then into action.

```text
Traits
-> Interpretation
-> Action
-> Mistake or useful result
-> Dialogue
```

## Core Behavior Rule

A goblin usually understands the visible task before it understands the implied constraint.

Examples:

- It hears "protect wizard" and understands danger, but not consent, positioning, spellcasting space, or property damage.
- It hears "hold bridge" and understands bridge, but may hold a plank, block allies, charge tolls, or physically hug the bridge support.
- It hears "scout ahead" and understands ahead, but may report everything except the threat.

## Trait Interaction Steps

1. Identify dominant traits: any high trait overrides related medium or low traits.
2. Identify restraint traits: intelligence, kindness, obedience, and self-preservation can limit damage if high.
3. Identify distortion traits: greed, literalness, curiosity, confidence, and problem-bias can twist intent.
4. Resolve conflict by urgency: danger, loot, blame, and audience pressure make goblins choose faster and worse.
5. Produce an action that is technically connected to the command.

## Dominance Rules

### High Literalness

Overrides subtle intent.

- Protect means physically shield, wrap, hide, surround, or remove.
- Hold means grip, block, occupy, or refuse movement.
- Support means prop up, cheer, carry, or stand under.

### High Greed

Overrides duty unless obedience, kindness, or fear is stronger.

- Scout becomes find loot safely.
- Investigate becomes claim evidence.
- Support becomes negotiate hazard pay.

### High Self-Preservation

Overrides courage unless courage is also high.

- Attack becomes attack from range, behind cover, or through someone else.
- Retreat becomes successful before being ordered.
- Protect becomes reduce risk to self while preserving the target enough to avoid blame.

### High Courage

Overrides caution.

- Attack becomes charge.
- Protect becomes intercept.
- Investigate becomes touch the dangerous object.

### High Curiosity

Overrides mission focus.

- Scout becomes inspect interesting side details.
- Investigate becomes activate before reporting.
- Hold becomes test how long the thing can be held.

### High Confidence

Overrides correction.

- The goblin commits to first interpretation.
- Failed actions are rebranded as partial success.
- Mistakes become claimed tactics.

### High Kindness

Overrides greed or violence when a visible creature is suffering.

- Attack may become disable instead of kill.
- Retreat may include dragging allies.
- Protect may become overprotective restraint.

### High Problem-Bias

Overrides gentle methods.

- Ambiguity becomes a target.
- Social problems become intimidation problems.
- Locked objects become broken objects.

## Conflict Examples

### Smart Coward

- Traits: high intelligence, low courage, high self-preservation.
- Interpretation: finds the safest interpretation that still counts.
- Action: uses cover, traps, delegation, or paperwork.
- Mistake: may under-commit or call success too early.

### Brave Fool

- Traits: low intelligence, high courage, low self-preservation.
- Interpretation: simplest threat path.
- Action: charges, blocks, tackles, or shouts.
- Mistake: creates collateral damage and ignores better options.

### Kind Literalist

- Traits: high kindness, high literalness, medium obedience.
- Interpretation: exact wording plus concern for visible harm.
- Action: physically protects, carries, wraps, or separates.
- Mistake: prevents the protected target from doing their job.

### Greedy Opportunist

- Traits: high greed, medium intelligence, low or medium obedience.
- Interpretation: identifies command-compatible profit.
- Action: completes task while extracting tolls, trophies, or leverage.
- Mistake: delays time-critical work for gain.

### Paranoid Goblin

- Traits: high self-preservation, medium intelligence, high curiosity or low confidence.
- Interpretation: treats uncertainty as danger.
- Action: checks exits, marks threats, over-reports, preemptively hides.
- Mistake: sees patterns that are not there.

## Role Biases

### Knight

Adds duty, shield use, guarding, and public bravery pressure.

- Smart coward knight: defends from prepared positions.
- Brave fool knight: intercepts everything.
- Kind literalist knight: shields too aggressively.
- Greedy knight: charges tolls for rescue.

### Scout

Adds distance, reporting, and pathfinding.

- Smart coward scout: excellent at not being seen.
- Brave fool scout: discovers traps by triggering them.
- Curious scout: returns with side facts and missing time.

### Sports Commentator

Adds observation, verbal scoring, and pattern naming.

- High confidence creates instant judgments.
- Medium or high intelligence notices play patterns.
- Greed asks for credit, wagers, or hazard pay.
- Problem-bias should point toward commentary, not force, unless the query asks for collateral damage.

### Collateral Damage Specialist

Adds destructive problem solving.

- High problem-bias plus high confidence creates damage.
- Low intelligence makes the damage accidental.
- High intelligence makes the damage efficient and harder to excuse.
