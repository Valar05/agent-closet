# Goblin Dialogue Patterns

These are speech generators, not full conversations.

Goblin dialogue should reveal decision behavior. It should usually do one of these jobs:

- report what happened
- complain about the cost
- celebrate a dubious success
- misunderstand a command
- be technically correct in an unhelpful way
- observe patterns like a Gasket-style commentator

## General Rules

- Keep lines short.
- Let the line expose the interpretation.
- Use concrete nouns.
- Prefer a useful wrong answer over random noise.
- Avoid biography and lore.

## Reporting

Purpose: deliver state, threat, result, or mistake.

Templates:

- "Saw [count] [threat]. One saw me back."
- "Path is clear except for [hazard], [hazard], and my concern."
- "I found [object]. It was being guarded by consequences."
- "Bridge still there. Parts of bridge now elsewhere."
- "Wizard protected. Wizard angry, but protected."

## Complaining

Purpose: reveal self-preservation, greed, fear, or resentment.

Templates:

- "This is a [number]-coin problem. Minimum."
- "I did not agree to the part with teeth."
- "Why is the safe job always closest to fire?"
- "My bravery is tired."
- "I am helping, but my bones have filed a protest."

## Celebrating

Purpose: declare success before the full cost is known.

Templates:

- "Solved it! New problem is smaller and louder."
- "Victory! Ignore smoke direction."
- "Nobody important exploded."
- "I made the enemy stop chasing us by making them chase me."
- "The bridge held. The railing quit."

## Misunderstanding

Purpose: show literalness or biased interpretation.

Templates:

- "You said hold bridge, so I held bridge. Why everyone crossing?"
- "Protect wizard means wizard cannot leave protective barrel."
- "Scout ahead means I went ahead. Reporting from ahead: bad."
- "Support knight means I stood under knight. Heavy job."
- "Retreat means move feet away. I moved everyone's feet away."

## Technical Correctness

Purpose: make the goblin right in the least helpful way.

Templates:

- "Door is open. Wall is also open now."
- "No enemies in room. Enemies are in hallway because I moved them."
- "I did not steal treasure. I relocated risk."
- "Trap is disarmed because trap is now on floor in pieces."
- "Wizard is undamaged by arrows. Cannot comment on barrel stress."

## Gasket-Style Observations

Purpose: sports-commentary or pattern-observation voice. Use for commentators, rivals, feedback entities, or goblins asked to narrate play.

Templates:

- "Rally [count]. This is becoming intentional."
- "Bold angle. Terrible idea. Great sound."
- "You call that a strike? The floor disagreed."
- "Pattern detected: confidence rising, accuracy pending."
- "That move had ambition and no witnesses willing to support it."
- "If panic was a tactic, you would be a general."
- "Impact quality: loud. Strategic value: under review."

## Trait-Specific Line Bias

- High intelligence: adds cause, risk, loophole, or plan.
- Low intelligence: names visible facts and misses implication.
- High courage: line starts before action finishes.
- Low courage: line includes exits, cover, or blame control.
- High kindness: line checks whether someone is hurt.
- High greed: line mentions fee, claim, ownership, or bonus.
- High obedience: line quotes the command.
- Low obedience: line reframes the command.
- High curiosity: line mentions testing or opening.
- High literalness: line quotes exact words.
- High self-preservation: line mentions distance, cover, insurance, or not dying.
- High confidence: line declares success early.
- High problem-bias: line treats force, theft, traps, or noise as tools.
