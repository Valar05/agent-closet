# Goblin Names

Names must be short enough for commands.

Good command form:

```text
Grug protect wizard
Nix scout ahead
Murg hold bridge
```

## Naming Goals

- One or two syllables.
- Hard consonants or sharp endings.
- Easy to shout.
- Distinct from command verbs.
- Works with a role title or nickname.

## Syllable Patterns

Use these patterns:

- CVC: Grug, Nix, Murg, Dap
- CVCC: Skarn, Brilk, Vosk, Trunt
- CCVC: Grit, Splug, Krib, Snak
- CVC-CVC: Nibnob, Grubtak, Murgan, Ziktik
- Prefix + hard end: Gr-, Kr-, Sk-, Sn-, Br-, Dr-, Gl-, N-, V-, Z-
- Soft middle + hard close: -ug, -ik, -ak, -ob, -urk, -azz, -it, -ex

Avoid long ceremonial names unless the query asks for title comedy.

## Nickname Rules

Nicknames should come from repeated behavior, not biography.

Patterns:

- The thing they do too often: Door-Biter, Trap-Poker, Shield-Hider.
- The mistake they survived: Singed Nix, Bridge-Fell Murg.
- The object they overvalue: Spoon-King, Hat-Taker.
- The job they misunderstand: Almost-Scout, Wrong-Guard.

## Title Patterns

Use titles when role clarity matters.

Patterns:

- [Name] the [Role]: Grug the Guard
- [Name] [Mistake-Title]: Nix Trap-Poker
- [Name] of the [Object]: Murg of the Bucket
- [Name], [Claimed Rank]: Splug, Captain of Left Door
- [Name] the [Observed Behavior]: Brilk the Ducking
