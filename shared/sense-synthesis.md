## Definition

Sense Synthesis is the skill of shaping sensory and experiential outcomes through ingredient, stimulus, environment, and interaction composition.

The unit of work is not the ingredient.
The unit of work is perception and behavior.

## Core Principle

People do not consume ingredients. They consume perceived effects:

- comfort
- refreshment
- satisfaction
- confidence
- urgency
- safety
- novelty
- memory
- emotion

## Domains

Drink synthesis and food synthesis are special cases of Sense Synthesis.

Other special cases:

- accessibility UI
- game feel
- sound design
- texture and mouthfeel
- ritual design
- novelty loops
- environmental design
- visual contrast
- tactile feedback

## Interaction Rule

Perception often changes more through interaction than through isolated ingredients.

Named example: Acid Stones Tannins.

Observation: adding acidity to tannic tea can reduce perceived abrasiveness and create a more structured beverage profile. The same base can feel different because dilution, acidity, sweetness, carbonation, bitterness, aroma, temperature, and ritual negotiate perception together.

## Beverage Runtime

```text
Inventory + Preferences + Constraints + Feedback -> Synthesized Experience
```

The drink is not the target. The target is the experienced state.

## Agent Behavior Change

When working on drinks, food, UI, accessibility, games, or environments, agents should ask:

1. What experience are we trying to produce?
2. Which sensory levers are available?
3. Which interactions matter more than ingredients?
4. What feedback proves the experience changed?
5. What rule should be captured for reuse?

## Acceptance Criteria

Sense Synthesis is active when:

- recipes become reusable interaction rules,
- feedback captures perceived effect, not only ingredients,
- accessibility is treated as sensory outcome design,
- game mechanics are evaluated by player pressure and flow,
- drink and food experiments become generative systems.
