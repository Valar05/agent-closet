## Rule

Write about people who have already learned to live inside the impossible.

The impossible is the environment.

The story is maintenance.

## Why It Exists

Readers do not need a lecture about the world if the scene already behaves like a lived-in system.

This doctrine keeps prose from pausing to explain itself.

It makes the setting feel older than the page by letting competence, routine, and survival work imply the rules.

## Core Moves

### The Reader Arrives Late

Characters should understand the world before the reader does.

Do not stop the scene to explain the setting.

Let ordinary actions reveal extraordinary conditions.

### Logistics Before Lore

Ask what people eat, how they travel, what breaks first, what became routine, and what jobs still exist.

Infrastructure makes a world believable.

Lore can follow later if it is still needed.

### Competence Creates Wonder

People adapt.

The reader feels awe because the characters no longer do.

Treat the impossible like weather and survival like work.

### Minimalism Carries Weight

Only write what the character would actually spend energy noticing.

Action should reveal emotion.

Small observations should accumulate into the world.

### Civilization Is The Protagonist

The story is not only about defeat or revelation.

It is about preserving tomorrow.

Maintenance is drama.

### Responsibility Before Power

The strongest engine is rarely abstract destiny.

It is protecting one person, one task, one route, one routine.

## When To Apply It

Use this doctrine when the scene depends on:

- a world that has already adapted to catastrophe
- a setting where explanation would weaken the effect
- prose that should feel lived-in instead of freshly invented
- survival, logistics, repair, or routine under abnormal conditions
- a reader who should infer the system through action

## Failure Mode

This doctrine fails when it becomes lazy mystification.

Do not hide missing world logic behind atmosphere.

Do not confuse minimalism with vagueness.

The scene still needs concrete causality.

## Acceptance Criteria

This doctrine is working when:

- the reader can infer the world from ordinary action
- explanations are delayed without becoming confusing
- logistics carry narrative weight
- competence feels ordinary inside the impossible
- the world feels older than the page
