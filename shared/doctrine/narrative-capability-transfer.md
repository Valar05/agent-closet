# Narrative Capability Transfer

## Rule

Stories do more than entertain.

They transfer judgment.

A well-written protagonist lets the reader inhabit a worldview from the inside long enough to understand why it exists.

The goal is not to win an argument.

The goal is to make a way of seeing portable.

## Why It Exists

Arguments tell the reader what to conclude.

Narrative lets the reader walk the road that produced the conclusion.

When the journey is honest, compressed truths that once sounded strange become understandable.

That is capability transfer.

## Core Moves

### Characters Become Lenses

Characters refract experience into a form another mind can inhabit.

The story is not cloning a personality.

It is transporting a usable mode of perception.

### Understanding Precedes Agreement

Readers do not need to agree with the character.

They only need to understand why the character believes what they do.

If the path is legible, the judgment can travel.

### Judgment Lives In Sequence

The reader should see what the character sees, then what they infer, then what they choose.

The sequence matters more than the slogan.

### Truth Becomes Portable Through Scene

If the scene is honest and compressed, the reader carries some of that perception after the story ends.

The result is not conversion by force.

It is transfer by experience.

## When To Apply It

Use this doctrine when the work depends on:

- a protagonist whose worldview must be felt, not merely stated
- a reader who needs to understand a conclusion without being preached at
- fiction that wants to teach judgment through sequence and consequence
- perspective work where internal logic matters more than debate
- stories that should leave the reader carrying a changed frame

## Failure Mode

This doctrine fails when the story becomes a lecture with costumes.

If the narrative only announces conclusions, it does not transfer capability.

If the protagonist is just a mouthpiece, the reader never walks the road.

## Acceptance Criteria

This doctrine is working when:

- the reader understands why the character believes what they do
- the story transmits a usable way of seeing
- conclusions feel earned through sequence
- the prose avoids turning into argument
- the reader leaves with part of the frame intact

## Example

A reader does not just learn that a character trusts maintenance over prophecy.

They watch the character live through the consequences, make the same practical choices, and arrive at the conclusion from inside the pressure.
