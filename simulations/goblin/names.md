# Goblin Names

Goblin names should be short, mouthy, memorable, and slightly unsafe to say near machinery.

They are not noble fantasy names. They are tool sounds, snack sounds, impact sounds, complaint sounds, and bad decisions with consonants.

## Core Examples

- Gribble
- Narg
- Splug
- Murg
- Blim

## Construction Patterns

### One-Syllable Impact Names

Pattern:

```text
hard consonant + short vowel + blunt ending
```

Examples:

- Narg
- Murg
- Blim
- Krub
- Gonk
- Plib
- Vrag

Use for:

- simple fighters
- bruisers
- guards
- loud idiots
- disposable commentators

### Two-Syllable Grubby Names

Pattern:

```text
grimy start + wobble middle + soft or clipped ending
```

Examples:

- Gribble
- Spluggo
- Nibnack
- Murgle
- Krattle
- Snorbin
- Glimbit

Use for:

- talkers
- mechanics
- recurring NPCs
- goblins with opinions

### Tool-Noise Names

Pattern:

```text
clank/snap/scrape sound adapted into a name
```

Examples:

- Clib
- Skritch
- Klanko
- Rattlebit
- Spannerk
- Grinds

Use for:

- engineers
- sappers
- gadget goblins
- Gasket-adjacent goblins

### Snack-and-Mud Names

Pattern:

```text
food/mud/body-noise implication without becoming gross canon
```

Examples:

- Crumbit
- Noodlegrub
- Sog
- Picklebit
- Gravy Narg
- Mudblim

Use for:

- comic goblins
- crowd goblins
- kitchen raiders
- sports fans

### Title Add-ons

Goblin titles should sound self-awarded, suspicious, or task-based.

Examples:

- Gribble the Almost Ready
- Narg Fence-Biter
- Splug of the Good Helmet
- Murg the Insurance Problem
- Blim Two-Sticks
- Skritch Door-Tester
- Crumbit Snack Captain

## Name Rules

Good goblin names:

- are easy to shout
- can be funny without explanation
- imply behavior
- fit in dialogue
- avoid elaborate apostrophes
- avoid tragic backstory gravity

Bad goblin names:

- sound too elven
- sound too heroic
- require pronunciation notes
- imply lore the simulation does not need
- are longer than the goblin's attention span

## Name Generation Formula

Use one of these:

```text
[Impact syllable]
[Grubby two-syllable name]
[Tool noise name]
[Food/mud modifier] + [impact name]
[Name] + [self-awarded title]
```

Examples:

```text
Narg
Gribble
Skritch
Mudblim
Splug the Probably Certified
```
