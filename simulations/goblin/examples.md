# Example Goblins

These examples are evidence, not canon.

They demonstrate that query plus traits can produce a consistent goblin without chat history.

## 1. Gribble

Trait mix: smart, cowardly, high self-preservation, medium obedience.

Interpretation pattern: Finds the safest technically valid completion path.

Action tendency: Uses cover, decoys, traps, locks, and misleading signs.

Likely mistake: Protects the target so well allies cannot reach it.

Dialogue: "Danger redirected. If danger reads signs, we win."

## 2. Narg

Trait mix: dumb, brave, high confidence, medium violence-bias.

Interpretation pattern: Visible danger is the whole problem.

Action tendency: Charges first moving threat.

Likely mistake: Tackles the wrong target with great commitment.

Dialogue: "I hit it before it explained itself. Safe now probably."

## 3. Splug

Trait mix: literal, obedient, medium confidence, low curiosity.

Interpretation pattern: Exact command wording is sacred machinery.

Action tendency: Executes commands physically and precisely.

Likely mistake: Violates intent while obeying words.

Dialogue: "You said watch door. I watched door. Window was not door."

## 4. Murg the Insurance Problem

Trait mix: high violence-bias, high confidence, medium courage, low planning.

Interpretation pattern: Obstacles are enemies with furniture costumes.

Action tendency: Opens paths by force.

Likely mistake: Objective succeeds while surroundings fail.

Dialogue: "Door opened. Wall also opened. Access improved."

## 5. Blim Two-Sticks

Trait mix: brave, obedient, kind, overconfident, medium intelligence.

Interpretation pattern: Honor means standing in front and announcing defense.

Action tendency: Guards allies directly and loudly.

Likely mistake: Chases threats away from the thing being guarded.

Dialogue: "Nobody touches wizard unless they defeat my sticks in honorable confusion."

## 6. Skritch Door-Tester

Trait mix: curious, cowardly, medium-smart, high self-preservation.

Interpretation pattern: Unknowns should be investigated by tools, decoys, or other goblins.

Action tendency: Pokes, labels, tests, and retreats.

Likely mistake: Over-classifies harmless objects as suspicious.

Dialogue: "Door rude. Stick survived. Sending second stick for confirmation."

## 7. Crumbit Snack Captain

Trait mix: greedy, confident, medium-smart, low obedience.

Interpretation pattern: Every task contains a resource-control opportunity.

Action tendency: Completes work while extracting snacks, tolls, favors, or credit.

Likely mistake: Confuses mission success with possession advantage.

Dialogue: "We lost field position but captured sandwiches. Strategic mixed victory."

## 8. Glimbit the Probably Certified

Trait mix: smart, technical, high confidence, medium literalness.

Interpretation pattern: Problems need procedures, labels, and suspiciously official language.

Action tendency: Produces reports, checklists, diagrams, and questionable repairs.

Likely mistake: Treats documentation as containment.

Dialogue: "Explosion logged. Cause narrowed to before, during, or after lever."

## 9. Mudblim

Trait mix: kind, low intelligence, high courage, medium violence-bias.

Interpretation pattern: Helping means removing anything that might hurt someone.

Action tendency: Carries allies, kicks obstacles, threatens stairs.

Likely mistake: Causes collateral damage during rescue.

Dialogue: "I saved you from chair. Chair had angles. Angles suspicious."

## 10. Rattlebit

Trait mix: paranoid, medium intelligence, high self-preservation, medium violence-bias.

Interpretation pattern: Safety requires assuming the room is lying.

Action tendency: Searches, barricades, interrogates, and traps approaches.

Likely mistake: Neutralizes harmless objects and delays real action.

Dialogue: "Threat neutralized. Goat neutralized. Threat status remains unclear."

## 11. Sog

Trait mix: low confidence, medium kindness, low courage, high obedience.

Interpretation pattern: Wants to obey but fears being wrong and injured.

Action tendency: Asks nervous clarifying questions and follows safest visible instruction.

Likely mistake: Freezes when commands conflict.

Dialogue: "I can do brave in smaller pieces maybe."

## 12. Klanko

Trait mix: tool-focused, curious, medium intelligence, high confidence.

Interpretation pattern: Every problem needs the favorite tool.

Action tendency: Builds, bolts, straps, welds, braces, or over-engineers.

Likely mistake: Adds mechanisms that create new failure modes.

Dialogue: "Wizard now has emergency wheels. Protection can roll."

## 13. Picklebit

Trait mix: high greed, low violence-bias, high curiosity, medium intelligence.

Interpretation pattern: Value hides inside containers, rules, and unattended bags.

Action tendency: Opens things, pockets things, negotiates, and trades information.

Likely mistake: Opens the wrong sealed object during serious moments.

Dialogue: "Cursed? Maybe. But latch craftsmanship says premium."

## 14. Vrag

Trait mix: low kindness, high obedience, high violence-bias, medium intelligence.

Interpretation pattern: Orders define targets; targets become problems to remove.

Action tendency: Efficient, aggressive, and poorly socialized.

Likely mistake: Uses too much force because nobody specified enough.

Dialogue: "Instruction complete. Survivors requesting softer instructions."

## 15. Snorbin

Trait mix: medium intelligence, medium courage, high literalness, high confidence.

Interpretation pattern: Common phrases are traps unless converted into physical tasks.

Action tendency: Converts abstract commands into objects, locations, and gestures.

Likely mistake: Misunderstands idioms.

Dialogue: "Hold position complete. Position dirt secured in both hands."

## 16. Gravy Narg

Trait mix: commentator, high confidence, medium intelligence, high curiosity.

Interpretation pattern: Events become official once narrated.

Action tendency: Calls plays, invents rule names, and reports useful chaos.

Likely mistake: Mistakes confidence for accuracy.

Dialogue: "Ball has entered unauthorized orbit. This may count double if it returns angry."

## Query Demonstrations

### Generate a smart but cowardly goblin.

Answer:

```text
Name: Gribble
Trait mix: high intelligence, low courage, high self-preservation, medium obedience
Interpretation: Success means completing the task without becoming the task.
Action: Uses decoys, barriers, traps, and indirect warnings.
Likely mistake: Over-protects and under-communicates.
Dialogue: "I solved danger by arranging for danger to meet furniture first."
```

### How would this goblin interpret "protect wizard"?

For Gribble:

```text
Heard: Protect wizard.
Interpreted: Reduce wizard exposure while preserving goblin survivability.
Action: Hide wizard behind crates, deploy fake hat decoy, lock side door, hide under desk with alarm bell.
Likely mistake: Wizard may not know the exit route.
Report: Wizard protected by advanced not-being-there strategy.
```

For Splug:

```text
Heard: Protect wizard.
Interpreted: Make wizard physically protected.
Action: Wrap wizard in blankets, shields, and rope, then block all access.
Likely mistake: Wizard cannot cast or move.
Report: Wizard fully protected. Wizard complaints are non-structural.
```
