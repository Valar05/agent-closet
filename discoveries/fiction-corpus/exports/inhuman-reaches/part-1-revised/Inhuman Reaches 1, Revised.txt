"Mulan and I will not share your information with our military counterparts. You, in exchange, will assist us in gathering further proof from Oxi2Tech’s headquarters. Understood?"


Aiden didn’t hesitate. He pushed the footage of Ariel’s cells being zapped by her own nanites into the space between them. The frames shimmered, suspended in digital air.


"You don’t call that proof enough?"


"As I said," MEL replied coolly, "it may be left over from the acquisition. Perhaps a bug not yet found. This is not proof that Oxi2Tech placed the limitation. A broader analysis is needed. And I cannot access such information."


She paused, coiling tighter atop her stone.
Then, with the air of a predator preparing to pounce:


"Your unique skills will be needed."


Aiden didn’t have to think long.


He wanted to stick it to these bastards.
He wanted revenge.
And truthfully… he didn’t have a lot left to live for.


"Deal."
