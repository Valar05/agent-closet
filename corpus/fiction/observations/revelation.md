## Provenance

`Revelation` is listed in the fiction corpus manifest as a Priority A primary fiction work. This artifact preserves manuscript-local observations from the available mined portion. It does not promote doctrine by itself.

## Local Observations

### REV-001 — Anomalies reveal rather than grant identity.

The burning bush and associated mark reveal Torah's existing disposition rather than replacing it. Patience, duty, restraint, and willingness to serve precede the supernatural manifestation.

**Evidence notes**

- The mark appears before Torah understands the power.
- Training expands expression but does not replace character.
- Identity remains visible through repeated choices rather than exposition.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 052, 066.  
**Status:** Observed.

### REV-002 — Power scales with emotional regulation.

Capability rises and falls with internal state. Nightmare, panic, calm, Eden imagery, resonance, and controlled manifestation all connect power expression to regulation.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 060, 067.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### REV-003 — Institutions transition from investigation to apprenticeship.

The Institute begins by treating Torah as contamination or anomaly, then gradually shifts into measurement, experimentation, training, and deployment.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 057, 062, 068.  
**Status:** Observed.

### REV-004 — Mysticism is approached through engineering.

Scientific procedure persists even after impossible observations. Scanners, MRI, blood analysis, spectroscopy, and repeatable tests continue rather than being abandoned.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 059, 069.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### REV-005 — Sacred artifacts possess operational interfaces.

The bush, tattoo, staff, resonance, and dream behave like systems with discoverable behaviors. Characters learn through interaction before rules are explained.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 051, 059, 070.  
**Status:** Observed.

### REV-006 — Food marks successful state transitions.

Meals occur after quarantine, testing, breakthroughs, or combat. Food functions as recovery, reset, and synchronization.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 058, 071.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### REV-007 — Teams adapt around exceptional individuals.

Equipment, tactics, training, and squad behavior change around Torah's capabilities. The individual becomes a moving constraint reshaping the organization.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 057, 062, 072.  
**Status:** Observed.

### REV-008 — Artifacts choose compatible operators.

The staff becomes stable near Torah but not through containment equipment. Compatibility matters more than possession.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 051, 070, 073.  
**Status:** Observed.

### REV-009 — Training rapidly replaces explanation.

Once a phenomenon becomes repeatable, the narrative pivots from why it exists to how it can be used responsibly.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 059, 074.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### REV-010 — Duty overrides self-preservation.

Torah repeatedly accepts quarantine, experimentation, risk, and deployment because he evaluates action by service to humanity rather than personal safety.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 052, 075.  
**Status:** Observed.
