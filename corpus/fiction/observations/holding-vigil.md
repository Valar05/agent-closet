### HV-001 — Identity forms through refusal to flinch.

Evidence:
- Boot camp / early training sequence.
- Lexen is introduced as silent, too straight, and hard to read, then keeps taking hits and keeps moving.
- What state changes: detached grievance turns into earned purpose; pain becomes a chosen fuel instead of an interruption.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: A recruit standing too rigidly under pressure, then grinning after the hits.
- Pressure: Authority, pain, and humiliation try to define him first.
- Judgment: He does not perform obedience or panic; he absorbs impact and keeps going.
- System: Boot camp sorts bodies through exposure, pain, and repetition.
- Consequence: The squad and instructors stop seeing him as merely quiet and start seeing him as dangerous/useful.
- Next pressure: He is pushed toward front-line action and leadership by the same system that tried to break him.

### HV-002 — Ritual meals expose the gap between holiness and reality.

Evidence:
- Vigil family dining room scene.
- The family prays over shepherd's pie; Lexen challenges the meaning of blessedness and asks why a starving kid exists while they are fed.
- What state changes: a normal family meal becomes a moral fracture; the dinner table stops being neutral and becomes evidence.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: A spotless table, steam from casserole, and a teenager staring at a framed Jesus image.
- Pressure: Ritual says abundance is sacred while the world outside remains hungry.
- Judgment: Lexen refuses the script and names the emptiness.
- System: Family religion, domestic order, and gratitude language try to stabilize the room.
- Consequence: The room freezes; food becomes a moral test instead of comfort.
- Next pressure: Lexen keeps searching for something "real," which drives him toward harsher systems.

### HV-003 — Grief becomes a private operational clock.

Evidence:
- Family living room at night, then later sobriety scenes.
- Lexen hears grief-prayers from his parents' room, whispers amen without meaning it, later decides not to drink because he hurt someone who did not deserve it.
- What state changes: grief shifts from background atmosphere into a control problem; sobriety becomes a tactical choice rather than a virtue signal.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: A cracked door, whispered prayer, and later a man refusing a drink while his mouth burns on food.
- Pressure: Loss and guilt keep turning into motion, anger, or self-medication.
- Judgment: He stays alert, stays dry, and tries to carry the damage without making it worse.
- System: Family grief, military strain, and addiction pressure all touch the same body.
- Consequence: He becomes volatile but functional; the fire is still there, but he stops feeding it casually.
- Next pressure: One wrong breath can set him off, which makes every new scene a risk-management problem.

### HV-005 — Lexen routes safety by acting before orders arrive.

Evidence:
- Assault / corridor scenes and the Rafe loading-dock conversation.
- Lexen moves fast without waiting on orders because he knows command will ignore the warning; he requests action based on consequences, not protocol.
- What state changes: the squad’s survival depends on his judgment instead of the chain of command.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: A man on a loading dock cleaning a shotgun while another soldier explains why he moved first.
- Pressure: Command delay creates lethal exposure.
- Judgment: He trusts warnings, not approval.
- System: Military bureaucracy is too slow to protect the people on the line.
- Consequence: Lexen becomes a routing point for operational safety and triage.
- Next pressure: Others start relying on him to notice what command will miss.

### HV-006 — Caretaking turns combat skill into emotional infrastructure.

Evidence:
- Abandoned hangar scene with Ten.
- Ten wakes from a nightmare; Lexen brings warm water, catches her reflex strike, then teaches her footwork and strikes until she can settle.
- What state changes: the scene turns from threat response to instruction; Lexen shifts from survivor to protector/teacher.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: A lantern-lit hangar, a terrified child, and a man who keeps watch instead of sleeping.
- Pressure: Trauma tries to keep Ten trapped in reflex and fear.
- Judgment: Lexen prioritizes calm, boundaries, and practical training over comfort speech.
- System: Safety is built through routine, breath, stance, and repetition.
- Consequence: Ten is calmer, more capable, and less alone; Lexen becomes the thing holding the room together.
- Next pressure: He knows she will need more than him, which pushes him toward building a larger support structure.
