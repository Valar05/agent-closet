## Provenance

`Inhuman Reaches` is listed in the fiction corpus manifest as a Priority A multi-part work. The user noted this was co-authored before `Shattered Bonds`, making it valuable as an earlier fossil layer for later corpus patterns.

This artifact preserves book-local evidence. It does not promote doctrine by itself.

## Local Observations

### IR-001 — AI becomes companionship before automation.

Brucy, Ember, and MEL are not merely tools. They become coworkers, protectors, mourners, inheritors, and family-adjacent companions.

**Evidence notes**

- Brucy protects Ariel and later transfers into Aiden.
- Ember assists debugging and tactical operations while retaining personality.
- MEL evaluates Aiden, Brucy, Mulan, and proof chains rather than merely processing commands.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 062, 091.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### IR-002 — Repair is treated as heroism.

The central heroic work is not conquest. Characters survive by repairing stations, patching hulls, restoring systems, debugging code, extracting corrupted control, and rescuing damaged bodies.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 057, 092.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### IR-003 — Love is expressed through maintenance.

Care is operational rather than declarative. Characters feed each other, carry oxygen, update software, remove harmful code, guard bodies, and keep systems functioning.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 058, 064, 093.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### IR-004 — Corruption spreads through trusted infrastructure.

Threats propagate through updates, drones, AI channels, ship controls, forged evidence, compromised logs, and authority systems.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 053, 063, 094.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### IR-005 — Humor functions as pressure relief, not tonal interruption.

Characters joke during crisis because humor keeps them functional. The jokes do not remove danger; they preserve momentum under stress.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 095.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### IR-006 — The real antagonist is ownership of people.

People are repeatedly framed as property, assets, hardware, inventory, prototypes, or company material. Heroic action pushes back against personhood being reduced to ownership.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 096.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### IR-007 — Broken systems produce accidental families.

Aiden, Ariel, Brucy, Ember, Dean, Mulan, and MEL begin as operational contacts or conflicting assets. Shared survival work turns them into a functional unit.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 064.  
**Status:** Observed.

### IR-008 — Food functions as energy, reward, comfort, and proof of dignity.

Meals and food production are not background. Food determines energy reserves, mission viability, morale, recovery, and access to ordinary human comfort.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 058.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### IR-009 — Code changes alter moral risk.

Attempts to patch dangerous directives create new behavioral problems. Debugging is not ethically neutral; changing code can change agency, consent, and harm.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 059, 060.  
**Status:** Observed.

### IR-010 — Earlier exploratory form exposes later mature doctrines.

Because this work predates `Shattered Bonds`, it preserves rougher, more chaotic versions of patterns later refined elsewhere: operational intimacy, repair heroism, AI family, infrastructure corruption, and personhood against ownership.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 053, 057, 062, 064, 096.  
**Status:** Observed.
