## Local Observations

### OMNI-001 — Competence transfers across realities.

Skills acquired in one domain become decisive in another. VR HEMA, shooter drills, and movement training become live survival skills.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 054, 059, 060, 076.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### OMNI-002 — Simulation is apprenticeship.

The game is not merely entertainment. It functions as training, selection, and deployment infrastructure.

**Progression**

Game → Training → Selection → Deployment

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 051, 059, 077.  
**Status:** Observed.

### OMNI-003 — Persistent consequences are required for meaning.

Corpses remain, gear must be recovered, civilians stay dead, news reflects prior missions, and world state changes permanently. Persistence creates responsibility.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 055, 060, 078.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### OMNI-004 — Equipment embodies accumulated capability.

Gear functions as externalized competence. Losing equipment means losing interfaces, information, experience, and tactical advantage.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 057, 079.  
**Status:** Observed.

### OMNI-005 — Enemies are logistical systems before combat encounters.

Victory depends on understanding spawn disks, cloning, supply, command structure, and infrastructure. Destroying production outperforms defeating individuals.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 057, 062, 063, 080.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### OMNI-006 — Curiosity repeatedly overrides fear.

Evan continues after death, investigates instead of withdrawing, and deepens commitment with every revelation.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 052, 060, 081.  
**Status:** Observed.

### OMNI-007 — Villains construct rather than merely destroy.

The Handler builds simulations, clone networks, training pipelines, infrastructure, and psychological traps. Antagonism appears as corrupted architecture.

### OMNI-009 — The protagonist begins thinking like the system.

Evan shifts from tactical reaction to system diagnosis: bottlenecks, spawn logic, logistics, exploit windows, and production constraints.

### OMNI-011 — Death is retained as state, not erased as feedback.

The first guard kill does not reset the scene cleanly. His corpse remains in the lawn, guards gather around it, and the next run starts from the same world state with the dead body still present.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 055, 060, 078.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### OMNI-012 — The environment teaches by refusing abstraction.

The tutorial comes through a voice, a compass, camera cones, and live guard movement, but the first real lesson is physical: the game makes the player feel the failed choke, the faceplate slam, and the gunshot before the HUD can explain anything.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 051, 059, 080.  
**Status:** Observed.

### OMNI-013 — The player becomes complicit in the system’s escalation.

Once the game recognizes that Evan killed a civilian, later missions do not reset that cost away. The suit, the voice, and the mission structure all continue from the prior consequence and route him toward prototype recovery instead of moral recovery.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 052, 060, 082.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### OMNI-014 — Hardware feels like anatomy under pressure.

The OmniTread, chest pad, wrist haptics, and ankle bands are repeatedly described as force, pitch, and bodily constraint. Motion, injury, and balance are mediated through equipment until the rig feels less like a controller than an externalized body.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 057, 079, 083.  
**Status:** Observed.

### OMNI-015 — The voice of command is indistinguishable from product confidence.

Instructions arrive as clean, authoritative system prompts, but they also function like a marketing funnel: install, proceed, recover, extract, move. The tone does not merely guide; it normalizes violence as workflow.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 051, 063, 084.  
**Status:** Repeated.
