## Direct Manuscript Observations

### LTA-001 — Breath control is the first form of discipline.
Evidence:
- Book 1, early training scenes: Ruben learns to compress hydrogen only by matching his breathing, holding still, and treating the gas like a body-state problem rather than a spell.
- The same logic carries into sparring and later power use, where breath timing determines whether he stabilizes, detonates, or loses control.
- State changes from instinctive panic to repeatable technique.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: Power is visualized as pressure, breath, and containment.
- Pressure: Training converts fear into measurable control.
- Judgment: Success depends on patience and self-regulation.
- System: Potentiate power behaves like a managed physiological system.
- Consequence: Bad timing causes injury, waste, or explosion.
- Next pressure: Can control be maintained under combat and team use?

### LTA-002 — Meals and sleep are treated as operational maintenance.
Evidence:
- Book 1 repeatedly pairs alarms, combat training, and classwork with ration bars, mess hall meals, and collapsing into sleep.
- Book 2 escalates the same pattern: Sienna pushes Alexis to eat, checks her suit, and uses food plus rest to stabilize her after the gravity burden and the nanite crisis.
- State changes from body strain to temporary recovery.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: Food appears as fuel, repair, and emotional grounding.
- Pressure: Bodies must be maintained before they can perform.
- Judgment: Refusing care is framed as self-harm, not toughness.
- System: Nutrition and rest are part of the dome’s survival loop.
- Consequence: Exhaustion makes crisis management worse.
- Next pressure: Can the crew preserve health under longer failure cascades?

### LTA-003 — Alexis is recast from burdened utility into a growing specialist.
Evidence:
- Book 2 opens with Alexis exhausted as the dome’s gravity source, then Ruben explicitly orders her to release gravity and stop being treated like a machine.
- He gives her the suit, names her Voidwalker, and takes her outside so she can learn in motion instead of being confined to a support room.
- State changes from trapped infrastructure to developing operator.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: A child in a purple suit becomes a live systems node.
- Pressure: Care and labor are in conflict.
- Judgment: The story argues against using a child as a stopgap.
- System: Responsibility can be expanded, but not extracted without cost.
- Consequence: The dome must adapt when Alexis is no longer held in reserve.
- Next pressure: Can she grow without being consumed by the role?

### LTA-004 — Zero gravity turns training into a freedom test rather than a spectacle.
Evidence:
- Book 2: Alexis first flails, overshoots, and corrects herself while Ruben coaches her through movement, braking, and confidence around the dome.
- The same zero-G environment later becomes a quiet recovery space where she can breathe, play, and feel "not heavy" for the first time in the book.
- State changes from disorientation to self-directed movement.

Compiler relevance:
- Image: Floating becomes a visual metaphor for relief.
- Pressure: Freedom only works if the body can self-correct.
- Judgment: The scene values learning over performance.
- System: Gravity is not just physics; it is emotional and social state.
- Consequence: Once Alexis can move, she can be trusted with larger work.
- Next pressure: Can that freedom survive responsibility and fear?
