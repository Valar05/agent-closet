## Provenance

`The Harroway Connection` is listed in the fiction corpus manifest as a Priority B fiction candidate. The user summarized it as “the sensuous oddball that morphs into agency fantasy.” This artifact preserves that reading as book-local evidence.

This file should support future decision-making about whether `The Harroway Connection` should be promoted from Priority B candidate to a primary corpus work.

## Local Observations

### THC-001 — Luxury functions as psychological anesthesia.

The Harroway offers perfect baths, ideal clothing, flawless meals, custom drinks, comfort, and beautiful spaces. These comforts reduce resistance. The prison is not merely locked; it is pleasant enough that escape becomes emotionally expensive.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 056, 097.  
**Status:** Observed.

### THC-002 — Agency begins with infrastructure mastery.

Jean does not defeat 717 by becoming physically stronger. She learns switchboards, routing, portals, connections, logistics, and transportation. Infrastructure mastery precedes heroism.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 059, 062, 098.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### THC-003 — The protagonist is promoted rather than transformed.

Jean's capabilities arrive through successful operation of the existing system.

**Progression**

Operator → Traveler → Mediator → Hunter → Commander

The institution gradually grants greater authority because she keeps handling responsibility.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 052, 055, 099.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### THC-004 — Hospitality functions as containment.

The hotel feeds, clothes, bathes, houses, and comforts Jean while removing her phone, suitcase, time, and external identity. Care and captivity are intertwined.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 056, 097.  
**Status:** Observed.

### THC-005 — Telephone infrastructure is literal world infrastructure.

The switchboard does not merely route calls. It moves people, places, spirits, and power. Communication infrastructure becomes geography.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 059, 062, 098.  
**Status:** Observed.

### THC-006 — Secrets behave as transactional currency.

The casino and confessional turn private truths into chips, leverage, bargains, and power. Inner life becomes spendable currency.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 061, 096.  
**Status:** Observed.

### THC-007 — Sensory seduction gradually becomes agency fantasy.

The story opens through perfume, food, dresses, alcohol, bathwater, warmth, silk, and atmosphere. It later turns those sensory systems into operational power: routing, siphoning, summoning, feeding the hotel, and organizing battle.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 058, 062, 097, 098.  
**Status:** Observed.

### THC-008 — The operator becomes steward of the ecosystem.

Jean moves from employee to system steward. By the final act, she routes allies, gathers factions, distributes power, feeds Management, rescues Tom, and coordinates the assault.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 065, 098, 099.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### THC-009 — Comfort can be a prison, but also a recovery technology.

Food, sunlight, coffee, croissants, wine, clothing, and rest are dangerous when they pacify Jean, but restorative when used deliberately before action.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 058, 097.  
**Status:** Observed.

### THC-010 — Love is tested by whether it restores agency.

Gladys frames possession as love. Jean restores Tom's voice, rest, and choice. The narrative distinguishes consuming attachment from agency-restoring care.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 064, 096.  
**Status:** Observed.

## Promotion Candidates

- Luxury as containment.
- Infrastructure mastery as the root of agency.
- Promotion ladder rather than mutation ladder.
- Communication systems as literal world systems.
- Love as agency restoration rather than possession.

## Inclusion Recommendation

Promote `The Harroway Connection` from Priority B candidate to active fiction observation target. It strongly corroborates cross-corpus patterns around agency, infrastructure, stewardship, food/sensory state changes, and personhood against possession.
