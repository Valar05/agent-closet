## Local Observations

### VLS-001 — Protagonists are transformed into infrastructure against their will.

Jonah is not uploaded as a player. He becomes Host architecture: hardware for other people to use.

**Evidence notes**

- Jonah is classified as `UserType: Host`.
- His brain becomes a server-like substrate for connected sessions.
- Employment status reduces him to hardware with no pay and no real end date.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 051, 065, 086.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### VLS-002 — Understanding is used as cruelty.

Jonah finally receives the answer to the mystery he cared about: why VR became so real. The answer is revealed only after it is too late for him to benefit from it.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 056, 087.  
**Status:** Observed.

### VLS-003 — Hope arrives as another person rather than another weapon.

The turning point is Erin reading the support ticket, believing Jonah enough to investigate, and witnessing him as a person inside a monster body.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 064, 088.  
**Status:** Observed.

### VLS-004 — Game mechanics are treated as natural law.
