This artifact preserves book-local observations. It does not promote doctrine by itself.

## Local Observations

### VLS-001 — Protagonists are transformed into infrastructure against their will.

Jonah is not uploaded as a player. He becomes Host architecture: hardware for other people to use.

**Evidence notes**

- Jonah is classified as `UserType: Host`.
- His brain becomes a server-like substrate for connected sessions.
- Employment status reduces him to hardware with no pay and no real end date.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 051, 065, 086.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### VLS-002 — Understanding is used as cruelty.

Jonah finally receives the answer to the mystery he cared about: why VR became so real. The answer is revealed only after it is too late for him to benefit from it.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 056, 087.  
**Status:** Observed.

### VLS-003 — Hope arrives as another person rather than another weapon.

The turning point is Erin reading the support ticket, believing Jonah enough to investigate, and witnessing him as a person inside a monster body.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 064, 088.  
**Status:** Observed.

### VLS-004 — Game mechanics are treated as natural law.

Respawns, quest markers, mutations, equipment, level gates, and target markers function as real environmental laws. Characters investigate them like physics.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 059, 063, 089.  
**Status:** Repeated.

### VLS-005 — Progression increases agency rather than raw power.

Sonar, harpoon, armor, shield mutations, communication, and information all expand Jonah's options. The power fantasy is really an agency fantasy.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 060, 090.  
**Status:** Observed.

### VLS-006 — The prison is designed to make personhood illegible.

Jonah is visible to players as a monster and target. His human interior is hidden behind game systems that incentivize hunting him.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 096.  
**Status:** Observed.

### VLS-007 — Communication restores humanity.

Sign language and support-ticket exchange become survival infrastructure. Jonah becomes a person again because someone can receive and respond to his signal.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 064, 088.  
**Status:** Observed.

### VLS-008 — Survival depends on changing the interpretation layer.

Jonah cannot merely fight hunters. He and Erin must understand why the system marks him, how zones and quests function, and what rules govern pursuit.

**Cross-links:** Corpus Observation 059, 062, 089.  
**Status:** Observed.
