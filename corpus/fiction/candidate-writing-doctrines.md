# Candidate Writing Doctrines

Status: candidate / not promoted
Source basis: cross-corpus fiction observation artifacts
Scope: reusable craft principles awaiting canonical promotion

## 1. Route Responsibility Through a Person

Write the protagonist as the node everyone else depends on for safety, judgment, or coordination. Their importance should be structural, not merely emotional.

Why it matters:

It turns leadership into a visible burden and makes the story's pressure legible at a glance.

## 2. Make Repair the Dramatic Act

Treat maintenance, debugging, feeding, calibration, and stabilization as the real heroics.

Why it matters:

The story feels earned because the character changes the state of the world by keeping it functional.

## 3. Teach Systems by Contact

Reveal rules through use, failure, and interaction before explanation.

Why it matters:

Readers learn in the same order the protagonist does, which makes the world feel embodied and discoverable.

## 4. Use Food and Rest as Plot Transitions

Let meals, drink, and sleep close one pressure state and open the next.

Why it matters:

The body becomes the plot's metronome instead of background scenery.

## 5. Let Relationships Start as Utility

Begin with a shared task, shared need, or shared survival problem. Let care emerge from repetition.

Why it matters:

Affection feels earned when it grows out of repeated usefulness.

## 6. Progress by Expanding Choice Space

Give characters more verbs, routes, and forms of coordination rather than only more power.

Why it matters:

Progression reads as agency, not just escalation.

## 7. Keep Consequences in the World

Do not erase failure cleanly. Preserve the knowledge, scars, and altered state that the failure produced.

Why it matters:

The reader trusts a world that remembers.

## 8. Make Comfort Suspicious

Treat relief, luxury, oblivion, and rest as forces with costs, not as neutral background goods.

Why it matters:

Comfort becomes dramatic when it can restore or erase agency.

## 9. Write Institutions as Coherent and Harmful

Give authority real motives and real logic. Make the damage arise from what the institution optimizes for.

Why it matters:

Conflict stays adult and credible instead of collapsing into cartoon villainy.

## 10. Make Recognition Matter

Use witnessing, naming, communication, and belief as actual rescue mechanisms.

Why it matters:

Personhood returns through connection, not just through victory.
