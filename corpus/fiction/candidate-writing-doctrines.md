# Candidate Writing Doctrines

Status: candidate / not promoted
Source basis: cross-corpus fiction observation artifacts
Scope: reusable craft principles awaiting canonical promotion

## 1. Route Responsibility Through a Person

Write the protagonist as the node everyone else depends on for safety, judgment, or coordination. Their importance should be structural, not merely emotional.

Why it matters:

It turns leadership into a visible burden and makes the story's pressure legible at a glance.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Holding Vigil
- Observation ID: HV-005
- Local note: Lexen routes safety by acting before orders arrive and becomes the fallback when command is too slow.
- Why this supports the pattern: The protagonist is not just important; others route survival through him.

## 2. Make Repair the Dramatic Act

Treat maintenance, debugging, feeding, calibration, and stabilization as the real heroics.

Why it matters:

The story feels earned because the character changes the state of the world by keeping it functional.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Inhuman Reaches
- Observation ID: IR-002
- Local note: Repair is treated as heroism through patching hulls, restoring systems, debugging code, and rescuing damaged bodies.
- Why this supports the pattern: The scene treats repair as the decisive action rather than as background support.

## 3. Teach Systems by Contact

Reveal rules through use, failure, and interaction before explanation.

Why it matters:

Readers learn in the same order the protagonist does, which makes the world feel embodied and discoverable.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Revelation
- Observation ID: REV-005
- Local note: Sacred artifacts possess operational interfaces, and characters learn through interaction before the rules are spelled out.
- Why this supports the pattern: Understanding arrives through contact, not lecture.

## 4. Use Food and Rest as Plot Transitions

Let meals, drink, and sleep close one pressure state and open the next.

Why it matters:

The body becomes the plot's metronome instead of background scenery.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Revelation
- Observation ID: REV-006
- Local note: Food marks successful state transitions after quarantine, testing, breakthroughs, or combat.
- Why this supports the pattern: Nourishment closes one scene state and resets the next.

## 5. Let Relationships Start as Utility

Begin with a shared task, shared need, or shared survival problem. Let care emerge from repetition.

Why it matters:

Affection feels earned when it grows out of repeated usefulness.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Shattered Bonds Online
- Observation ID: SBO-002
- Local note: Companion support is distributed across human, bird, and system, and the group becomes a support lattice through use.
- Why this supports the pattern: The relationship starts as functional scaffolding and then becomes emotional dependence.

## 6. Progress by Expanding Choice Space

Give characters more verbs, routes, and forms of coordination rather than only more power.

Why it matters:

Progression reads as agency, not just escalation.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Veil of Liquid Stars
- Observation ID: VLS-013
- Local note: Progression expands Jonah's choices instead of merely inflating his damage.
- Why this supports the pattern: The upgrade changes decision space, not just output.

## 7. Keep Consequences in the World

Do not erase failure cleanly. Preserve the knowledge, scars, and altered state that the failure produced.

Why it matters:

The reader trusts a world that remembers.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Omnitread
- Observation ID: OMNI-003
- Local note: Persistent consequences are required for meaning; corpses remain, gear must be recovered, and world state changes permanently.
- Why this supports the pattern: Failure persists as usable context instead of being reset away.

## 8. Make Comfort Suspicious

Treat relief, luxury, oblivion, and rest as forces with costs, not as neutral background goods.

Why it matters:

Comfort becomes dramatic when it can restore or erase agency.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Aegis of Victory
- Observation ID: AOV-005
- Local note: The story repeatedly tests comfort against memory and distinguishes honest rest from forgetting.
- Why this supports the pattern: Relief is never neutral; it can preserve continuity or erase it.

## 9. Write Institutions as Coherent and Harmful

Give authority real motives and real logic. Make the damage arise from what the institution optimizes for.

Why it matters:

Conflict stays adult and credible instead of collapsing into cartoon villainy.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Aegis of Victory
- Observation ID: AOV-004
- Local note: Authority is careful, rational, and still cruel when it pursues preservation through isolation and extraction.
- Why this supports the pattern: The harm comes from coherent optimization, not stupidity.

## 10. Make Recognition Matter

Use witnessing, naming, communication, and belief as actual rescue mechanisms.

Why it matters:

Personhood returns through connection, not just through victory.

Evidence anchors:

- Work: Veil of Liquid Stars
- Observation ID: VLS-010
- Local note: Being witnessed is the first real rescue because Erin believes Jonah and breaks the isolation logic.
- Why this supports the pattern: Recognition is treated as operational rescue, not decorative sympathy.
